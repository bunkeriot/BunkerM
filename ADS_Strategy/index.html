    BunkerM Enterprise — Marketing & Ads Strategy Roadmap

    Context: BunkerM reached 440 GitHub stars and 10k+ Docker pulls organically within the first few months of release. Reddit and LinkedIn have generated inbound contacts but no closed enterprise deals yet. This document outlines a phased, low-budget strategy to move from organic traction to repeatable enterprise sales.

    Situation Analysis

    Strengths

    • Strong organic signal: 440 stars and 10k+ Docker pulls in a few months is genuine product-market fit evidence, not manufactured growth
    • Open-source Community edition creates a self-serve pipeline — people try it before they ever talk to sales
    • Local LLM and AI chat features are genuinely differentiated in the MQTT management space
    • Single Docker container deployment is a strong enterprise selling point (no complex infrastructure, air-gap friendly)

    The Gap

    Inbound contacts exist but are not converting to paid deals. This is almost always a middle-of-funnel problem, not a top-of-funnel one. The internal champion at a company finds BunkerM, likes it, but cannot make the case to management without: - A clear Enterprise offering with defined deliverables - Proof that others are using it in production - Materials they can forward to their CTO or procurement team

    Fix the middle of the funnel first. Then spend on ads to fill the top.

    Target Buyers

    Do not try to sell to everyone. Focus on these profiles:

    Profile Where They Are What They Need to Hear
    OT / Automation Engineer LinkedIn, industry forums, PLC/SCADA communities Reliability, air-gap capability, no vendor lock-in
    IoT Architect at mid-market company GitHub, Hacker News, dev communities Open-source credibility, Docker-native, REST API quality
    IT / Security Manager in regulated industry LinkedIn, industry press Compliance readiness, audit trail, SOC integration
    System Integrator / VAR LinkedIn, partner networks Easy deployment for their clients, white-label potential, margins

    Priority geography: Western Europe first (France, Germany, Benelux, UK). These markets have strong IIoT adoption, GDPR-driven preference for self-hosted solutions, and shorter enterprise sales cycles than North America for SMB-to-mid-market.

    Priority sectors: 1. Manufacturing and industrial automation (largest MQTT user base) 2. Building automation and smart facilities 3. Healthcare and life sciences (air-gap, compliance angle) 4. Agriculture and agritech (offline capability, Raspberry Pi support) 5. Oil, gas, and utilities (regulated, high MQTT usage)

    Phase 0 — Fix the Funnel Before Spending (Week 1, Free)

    These actions cost nothing and will make every euro of ad spend more effective.

    0.1 — Capture Production Users

    Add a passive survey to the BunkerM dashboard (a dismissible banner or a card in the Settings page) that asks: "Using BunkerM in production? Tell us about your setup." Link to a free Tally or Typeform asking: - What sector are you in? - How many MQTT devices are you managing? - What made you choose BunkerM?

    This surfaces real testimonials, real use cases, and real company names to use as social proof.

    0.2 — Create the Enterprise One-Pager

    Before running any ad, you need a landing destination that converts enterprise visitors. Create a simple one-page PDF (and a matching section on bunkerai.dev) covering: - What BunkerM Enterprise includes vs Community - Deployment options: on-premises, air-gapped, cloud VM - Security and compliance posture: TLS, ACL, audit trail, SOC-ready integrations - Support terms: response time SLA, dedicated onboarding - Reference deployments (anonymized is fine: "European food manufacturer, 200 devices") - How to get a quote: one button, links to sales@bunkerai.dev

    0.3 — GitHub Discussions Activation

    Post a thread in GitHub Discussions: "Are you running BunkerM in production? Share your setup." This creates social proof visible to every future visitor, surfaces real use cases, and often generates inbound from people who were lurking.

    Phase 1 — LinkedIn Before Anything Else (Weeks 2-4, Budget: €200-300 test)

    LinkedIn is more expensive per click than Facebook but your conversion rate for enterprise will be 5-10x higher because you can target by job title, industry, and company size simultaneously. Start here.

    Campaign Setup

    Objective: Lead generation (LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms, not website traffic — removes the friction of a landing page)

    Targeting: - Job titles: OT Engineer, Automation Engineer, IoT Architect, Infrastructure Engineer, CTO, VP Engineering, Head of IT, Systems Integrator - Industries: Manufacturing, Oil and Gas, Utilities, Healthcare, Construction and Facilities - Company size: 50 to 500 employees - Geography: France, Germany, Netherlands, Belgium, UK — test one country first (Germany or France) - Language: match the country

    Ad formats that work for developer tools on LinkedIn: 1. Single image ad with a dashboard screenshot and a short headline: "Manage 10,000 MQTT devices with one command. Open-source, self-hosted, AI-powered." 2. Short video (60-90 seconds) showing the AI chat controlling a device with plain English — this is the most differentiating visual you have 3. Document ad: share the Enterprise one-pager as a native LinkedIn document — high engagement for technical audiences

    Budget recommendation: - Start with €200 over 2 weeks - Set a daily cap of €15-20 - Optimize for Lead Gen Form opens, not clicks - After 2 weeks, evaluate cost per lead. Under €30 per lead is good for this audience. Under €15 is excellent.

    Content to Run in Parallel (Organic, No Cost)

    Post these on your personal LinkedIn and the BunkerM page during the campaign period to reinforce paid exposure: - "440 GitHub stars in 3 months — what we learned building an open-source MQTT platform" (milestone post with social proof) - Short screen recording of the AI chat: "Turn off the conveyor belt" being executed with no topic knowledge required - One customer story post, even if lightly anonymized: "A factory in [country] is managing X devices with BunkerM — here is what their setup looks like"

    Phase 2 — Facebook and Instagram for Retargeting Only (Weeks 3-8, Budget: €50-100/mo)

    Do not run cold Facebook ads for enterprise B2B software. The audience is wrong and the intent is wrong. Use Facebook exclusively for retargeting people who have already shown interest.

    Setup

    1. Install the Facebook Pixel on bunkerai.dev (one script tag in the Layout header)
    2. Create a Custom Audience: "People who visited /pricing or /docs/bunkerai in the last 30 days"
    3. Run a retargeting ad with a softer CTA than the LinkedIn lead gen: "Still evaluating BunkerM for your team? Book a 20-minute demo." Link to a Calendly.

    This keeps you top of mind for the 90% of visitors who did not convert on first visit. Facebook retargeting to a warm audience (your own website visitors) typically costs €0.50 to €2 per click — very cheap for the quality.

    Facebook Cold Ads — When to Use Them

    Facebook cold ads can work for the Community/hobbyist segment, not enterprise. If you want to grow Docker pulls and GitHub stars faster, a €50-100 Facebook campaign targeting: - Interests: MQTT, IoT, Home Assistant, Raspberry Pi, Docker, self-hosted software - Age: 25-45 - Geography: US, UK, Germany, France, Australia

    ...will grow top-of-funnel Community adoption, which feeds the enterprise pipeline organically over time.

    Phase 3 — Content Marketing for Inbound (Ongoing, Free)

    Paid ads rent attention. Content owns it. Three article types that reliably drive enterprise inbound for infrastructure tools:

    Type 1 — Sector-Specific Deployment Guides

    Write one article per target sector showing a realistic deployment scenario. Examples: - "MQTT management in a food processing facility: traceability, access control, and anomaly detection with BunkerM" - "Smart building automation with BunkerM: managing 500 MQTT endpoints in a commercial property" - "Air-gapped MQTT infrastructure for healthcare IoT: a BunkerM deployment guide"

    When an OT engineer at a food company Googles "MQTT management food processing", they find you. This is high-intent traffic that converts without any ad spend.

    Type 2 — Comparison and Evaluation Content

    Target people actively in the decision phase: - "Self-hosted MQTT broker management vs cloud IoT platforms: what changes at scale" - "Eclipse Mosquitto alone vs BunkerM: what you gain with a management layer" - "BunkerM vs HiveMQ vs EMQX: open-source MQTT management compared"

    Type 3 — The AI Differentiation Angle

    This is your most unique positioning. Nobody else has plain-English device control over MQTT at this price point. - "Controlling industrial equipment with plain English over MQTT — no payload knowledge required" - "How AI-assisted MQTT management reduces configuration errors in production environments"

    Publish one article per week on the blog at bunkerai.dev and cross-post to LinkedIn and Reddit (r/homeautomation, r/selfhosted, r/MQTT, r/iot).

    Phase 4 — System Integrators as a Force Multiplier (Month 2+)

    One system integrator can bring 10 enterprise clients. This channel is often ignored by early-stage software companies.

    How to Identify Them

    Search LinkedIn for "IoT system integrator [country]" or "industrial automation consultant". Look for companies with 5-50 employees that already work with MQTT-based systems.

    The Pitch to Integrators

    • BunkerM is free to deploy for their clients (Community edition)
    • They can add a management layer on top of existing Mosquitto deployments
    • Enterprise plan gives their clients a supported, compliant product with SLA
    • Consider a reseller margin or referral fee on Enterprise deals they bring in

    Activation

    Reach out to 5-10 integrators per week with a personalized LinkedIn message (not a template blast). Reference a specific sector they work in. Offer a free 30-minute technical walkthrough.

    Budget Recommendation — Starting Small

    Phase Channel Monthly Budget Expected Outcome
    Phase 0 Funnel fixes (Tally, one-pager) €0 Better conversion on existing traffic
    Phase 1 LinkedIn Lead Gen (test) €200 one-time 5-15 qualified leads to evaluate
    Phase 2a Facebook Pixel + retargeting €50/mo Top-of-mind for warm visitors
    Phase 2b Facebook cold (Community growth) €50/mo More Docker pulls and GitHub stars
    Phase 3 Blog content €0 (time only) Compounding inbound over 3-6 months
    Phase 4 Integrator outreach €0 Highest ROI channel if it lands

    Total to start: €300 one-time + €100/mo ongoing

    This is genuinely minimal. The goal of the first spend is not revenue — it is learning. Which job titles click? Which sectors respond? Which ad creative gets leads? Spend €300, get data, then scale what works.

    Tracking and Success Metrics

    Set these up before spending a single euro on ads:

    Metric Tool Target
    Website visitors by source Google Analytics 4 (free) Baseline first 2 weeks, then compare
    Leads from LinkedIn LinkedIn Campaign Manager Cost per lead under €30
    Demo bookings Calendly (free tier) 2-5 per month to start
    Community to Enterprise conversion Manual tracking in a spreadsheet 1 enterprise inquiry per 200 Community installs
    GitHub stars growth rate GitHub Insights Maintain 30+ new stars per month
    Docker pulls growth Docker Hub Maintain 10-20% month-over-month growth

    Key Principles to Keep in Mind

    • Fix the funnel before filling it. Ads on a leaky funnel waste money. The one-pager and demo booking flow must exist before you run a single ad.
    • Enterprise sales are slow. A deal that starts with a LinkedIn ad in week 2 may close in month 4. Consistency matters more than bursts.
    • The Community edition is your best sales rep. Every person who installs BunkerM is a potential enterprise buyer or a referral source. Keep improving it.
    • Your AI features are your moat. No other self-hosted MQTT manager has plain-English device control. Lead with this in every ad, post, and pitch.
    • Respond fast to every inbound. Speed of response is the single highest-ROI action in early enterprise sales. A reply within 2 hours beats a polished pitch 3 days later.
